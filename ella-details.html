Ella

Helping international student women manage hormonal balance and cycle irregularities.

Project Overview

Goal: A Menstrual Health App for International Female Students.

My Role: UX/UI Designer & Researcher

Tools Used: Figma, Zoom, Research Methods

Process: The project involved user research, wireframing, prototyping, and usability testing. I work collabratively on each part.

Step 1: Research

Our team conducted extensive research to understand the challenges faced by international female students regarding menstrual health. We gathered insights through **user interviews, surveys, and secondary research** to ensure our solutions were data-driven and user-centered. The research focused on three main areas:

  • Understanding User Needs: We interviewed international female students to identify the issues they faced due to relocation, including menstrual cycle irregularities, access to healthcare, and cultural stigmas around discussing menstrual health.
  • Survey Analysis: We conducted surveys across diverse groups to gather quantitative data on menstrual tracking habits, stress levels, and healthcare access. This helped us validate the common pain points and prioritize key features.
  • Competitor Research: We analyzed existing menstrual health apps to assess their strengths and weaknesses. This allowed us to design unique features tailored to international students, such as culturally sensitive support and localized healthcare resources.

Our findings reinforced the need for a **comprehensive, easy-to-use solution** that combines cycle tracking, mental health support, and a strong community for international female students. These insights directly shaped our design approach and feature development.

Step 2: Pre-Work

Before moving into wireframing and prototyping, we conducted essential pre-work to better understand user needs and shape our solution. This stage involved three key activities:

Empathy Map

We created an Empathy Map to capture user thoughts, feelings, and behaviors related to menstrual health. This helped us identify emotional triggers, challenges, and expectations from an app that would support international female students.

Road Map

We developed proto personas based on user research to represent our target audience. For example, Fang, a South Korean student, faces challenges in adjusting to a new healthcare system, struggles with hormonal changes, and needs an app that provides localized health resources and menstrual tracking features. Personas like this guided our feature decisions.

Personas

A roadmap was created to structure our design process. This collaborative session allowed us to outline key milestones, prioritize tasks, and ensure the project stayed aligned with our research findings. The roadmap provided a clear timeline for developing wireframes, prototypes, and usability tests.

Step 3: Wireframing

Once we had a clear understanding of user needs, we moved on to wireframing. This step involved:

  • Sketching Initial Ideas: We brainstormed layout structures for an intuitive and accessible user interface.
  • Creating Low-Fidelity Wireframes: Basic wireframes were designed in Figma to map out the app’s navigation and core features.
  • Gathering Feedback: We conducted quick reviews to refine the wireframes before proceeding to prototyping.

This stage ensured we had a solid foundation for usability before investing time into detailed designs.

Key Features

  • Real-time water quality data from verified sources
  • User-friendly map interface for locating water stations
  • Crowdsourced feedback and water quality rankings
  • Notifications and alerts for water resources
